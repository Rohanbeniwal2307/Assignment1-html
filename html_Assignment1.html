Exotic dance moves

Yes you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. To answer your question, my favourite dances are as follows with definitions:

polynesian chicken dance

A little known but very influential dance dating back as far as 300BC, a whole village would dance around in a circle like chickens, to encourage their livestock to be "fruitful".

Icelandic brownian shuffle

Before the icelanders developed fire as a mean of getting warm, they used to practice their dance, which involved huddling close together in a circle on the floor, and shuffling their bodies around imperceptibly tiny, very rapid movements. One of my fellow student use to say that he thought this dance inspired modern style such as Twerking.

Arectic robot dance

an interesting example of historic misinformation, English explorers in the 1960s believed to have discovered a new dance style characterised by "robotic" stilled movements, being practiced by inhabitants of Northern Alaska and canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this because they were really cold.
